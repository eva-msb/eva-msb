EMBODIED EMOTION: DANCE

Embodied emotion // mood regulation // emotion perception // dance

This line of research explores how emotional experience is expressed, perceived, and regulated through full-body movement. Across several projects, we show that imitating affectively expressive dance movements can shift mood states, whether modeled by human or robot avatars. We furhter developed validated full-body stimulus sets, enabling controlled creation, motion-capture recording, and kinematic analysis of emotional movement sequences. We show that movement kinematics, expressive intent, and individual traits shape aesthetic experience and engagement. Together, this line of work positions dance as a powerful lens for understanding how emotion is embodied, communicated, and transformed through the moving body.
